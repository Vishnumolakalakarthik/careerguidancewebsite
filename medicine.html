Dedicate your life to healthcare and healing

What is Medicine?

Medicine is the science and practice of caring for a patient, managing the diagnosis, prognosis, prevention, treatment, palliation of their injury or disease, and promoting their health. Medicine encompasses a variety of health care practices evolved to maintain and restore health by the prevention and treatment of illness.

Contemporary medicine applies biomedical sciences, medical technology to diagnose, treat, and prevent injury and disease, typically through the use of pharmaceuticals or surgery, but also through therapies as diverse as psychotherapy, external splints and traction, medical devices, biologics, and ionizing radiation, amongst others.

Medical Specializations

Medicine is a diverse field with many specialized branches. Here are some popular medical specializations you can consider:

MBBS (Allopathy)

The primary medical degree that allows practice of modern medicine, diagnose diseases, prescribe medications and perform surgeries.

BDS (Dentistry)

Focuses on oral health, dental procedures, oral surgeries, and prevention of dental diseases.

BAMS (Ayurveda)

Traditional Indian system of medicine that focuses on natural healing, herbs, and lifestyle management.

BHMS (Homeopathy)

A medical system that uses highly diluted doses of natural substances to stimulate the body's healing mechanisms.

B.Pharm (Pharmacy)

Involves the science of preparing and dispensing medications, drug research, and pharmaceutical care.

B.V.Sc (Veterinary Science)

Focuses on animal health, disease prevention, and medical treatment for animals.

Eligibility & Requirements

To pursue a career in medicine, you need to meet certain educational criteria and develop specific skills:

Educational Requirements:

  • 10+2 Qualification: Completion of 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as core subjects with a minimum of 50-60% marks (varies by institution)
  • Entrance Exams: Qualifying in national or state-level medical entrance exams like NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)
  • For Higher Studies: NEET-PG, AIIMS PG, JIPMER PG for postgraduate specializations

Skills Required:

  • Academic Excellence: Strong foundation in biology, chemistry, and other sciences
  • Compassion & Empathy: Ability to connect with patients and understand their concerns
  • Critical Thinking: Making quick and accurate decisions in emergency situations
  • Communication Skills: Explaining complex medical concepts to patients in simple terms
  • Stamina & Stress Management: Ability to work long hours and handle high-pressure situations
  • Ethical Awareness: Adhering to medical ethics and patient confidentiality

Medical Education Pathway

A typical pathway for medical education in India follows these steps:

1. Undergraduate Medical Education (5.5 years)

  • MBBS: 4.5 years of academic study + 1 year of compulsory rotating internship
  • BDS: 4 years of academic study + 1 year of compulsory rotating internship
  • BAMS/BHMS/BUMS: 4.5 years of study + 1 year of internship

2. Postgraduate Medical Education (2-3 years)

  • MD (Doctor of Medicine): 3 years - Clinical specialties like Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, etc.
  • MS (Master of Surgery): 3 years - Surgical specialties like General Surgery, Orthopedics, etc.
  • Diploma: 2 years - Specialized diplomas in various fields

3. Super-specialization (3 years)

  • DM (Doctorate of Medicine): Super-specialization in medical fields like Cardiology, Neurology, etc.
  • MCh (Master of Chirurgical): Super-specialization in surgical fields like Neurosurgery, Cardiothoracic Surgery, etc.

₹8 - 18 lakhs per annum (private sector)

₹15 - 40 lakhs per annum (varies by specialization)

Top Employers:

Government hospitals, private hospital chains like Apollo, Fortis, Max Healthcare, pharmaceutical companies, research institutions, international health organizations like WHO, and academic institutions
